Huawei Mate XT Review: The World’s First Tri-Fold Smartphone

Overview

Huawei Mate XT Ultimate Design is the world's first commercial tri-fold smartphone, redefining mobile design and multitasking. It offers a seamless fusion of smartphone portability and tablet productivity. Packed with flagship hardware, a groundbreaking foldable display, and Huawei’s latest innovations, the Mate XT stands as a premium showcase of what the future of mobile technology looks like.

Design and Build

Mate XT introduces a tri-fold design with aerospace-grade titanium frame and second-gen Kunlun Glass for maximum durability. When fully unfolded, it reveals a 10.2-inch OLED display with 2480 × 2225 resolution and 120Hz refresh rate. It supports HDR10+ and offers a near bezel-less experience.

Folded, the phone offers two outer displays for basic usage without opening the device. Thanks to its Z-shaped folding mechanism, users can switch between compact, dual-view, or full-screen tablet modes depending on the task. Despite its complex structure, it remains surprisingly slim and elegant in hand.

Performance and Hardware

Powered by Huawei's in-house Kirin 9000S chipset based on 7nm architecture, the Mate XT delivers flagship-grade performance. Paired with 16GB RAM and up to 1TB UFS 4.0 storage, it handles everything from gaming to productivity apps with ease.

Despite not having Qualcomm or MediaTek chips due to trade restrictions, Huawei’s chip performs well for multitasking, AI tasks, and EMUI-based features. The phone also supports 5G, Wi-Fi 6, and advanced cooling systems for sustained performance.

Camera Capabilities

Huawei continues its camera excellence with a triple Leica-tuned system:

  • 50MP main sensor (f/1.6, OIS)
  • 12MP ultra-wide camera
  • 8MP periscope telephoto (5x optical zoom)
Thanks to the tri-fold design, the outer screen can act as a viewfinder, letting users take high-quality selfies with the main camera. The device also supports 4K video, advanced night mode, AI scene detection, and ultra-slow motion capture.

Battery and Charging

Despite the large screen and tri-fold mechanism, the Mate XT packs a 5000mAh battery optimized for power efficiency. It supports 88W wired SuperCharge, 50W wireless charging, and reverse wireless charging.

AI-based power management learns your usage habits to extend screen-on time. With normal use, the battery lasts a full day, even when switching between fold states frequently.

Software and Features

Running on HarmonyOS 4.0, the Mate XT is fine-tuned for foldable multitasking. It supports:

  • Smart Multi-Window
  • Floating windows and drag-to-snap
  • Optimized app continuity across screens
Huawei’s ecosystem apps like Petal Search, AppGallery, and Petal Maps replace Google services. AI features such as real-time transcription, smart translation, and document scanning are deeply integrated into the OS.

Durability Improvements

The Mate XT is built to last. Its hinge system is lab-tested to survive over 300,000 folds. The Kunlun Glass used on all display surfaces improves drop resistance by up to 10x compared to conventional glass.

Though not IP-certified for full water resistance, the phone resists splashes and dust, making it reliable for everyday use.

Price and Availability

The Huawei Mate XT Ultimate Design is priced at ~$2,750 (approx.), making it one of the most expensive foldables on the market. It is available in limited markets like China, UAE, and select regions in Europe. Color options include:

  • Red Gold (Ultimate Edition)
  • Titanium Gray
  • Obsidian Black
    • Limited availability and high price position this device as a luxury item for tech collectors and professionals.

Conclusion

Huawei Mate XT isn’t just a smartphone — it’s a vision of the future. With its tri-fold display, powerful specs, luxury design, and HarmonyOS enhancements, it redefines the limits of mobile innovation. While its price and limited availability won’t suit everyone, it undoubtedly showcases Huawei’s leadership in foldable technology. If you're looking for the most futuristic phone money can buy, the Mate XT is it.
