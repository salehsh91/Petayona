Galaxy Z Tri-Fold

Samsung's Flagship Foldable for 2025: Full Review of Galaxy Z Tri-Fold

Overview

The Samsung Galaxy Z Tri-Fold is the company's 2025 flagship multi-fold smartphone, offering a premium foldable experience with a 10-inch internal display. Officially launched in December 2025, it targets users who demand productivity, immersive media, and advanced multitasking in a single device. With its dual-hinge design and cutting-edge hardware, the Tri-Fold combines innovation, luxury, and practical usability for tech enthusiasts and professionals alike.

Design and Build

The Galaxy Z Tri-Fold features a sleek, triple-panel foldable design, reinforced with Samsung’s Armor Aluminum frame and Gorilla Glass Victus on all surfaces. Its dual-hinge mechanism ensures smooth folding and long-term durability. Despite being a large foldable, Samsung managed to keep the closed device slim and manageable, though it weighs approximately 309 grams. The premium finish and attention to detail provide a sense of luxury uncommon in foldable devices.

Display

The main internal display is a 10.0-inch Dynamic AMOLED 2X panel with 2160×1584 resolution and a 120Hz refresh rate, delivering crisp visuals, vibrant colors, and smooth scrolling. The external 6.5-inch cover display also uses Dynamic AMOLED 2X with 120Hz, suitable for quick tasks without unfolding. The device offers excellent multitasking capabilities, similar to a small tablet, but users should note some glare under direct sunlight due to the glossy surface.

Performance and Hardware

Powered by the Qualcomm Snapdragon 8 Elite for Galaxy and 16GB RAM, the Tri-Fold handles demanding apps, gaming, and multitasking with ease. Storage options include 512GB and 1TB UFS 4.1. With 5G connectivity, Wi-Fi 7, and advanced AI processing, the device offers a seamless, high-performance experience for both work and leisure.

Camera Capabilities

The Tri-Fold sports a triple-camera system: a 200MP primary sensor, 12MP ultrawide, and 10MP telephoto with 3× optical zoom. Two 10MP front cameras handle selfies on both the main and cover display. Video recording supports up to 8K at 30fps. While impressive on paper, early reviews suggest the camera quality may not always surpass top-tier standalone flagship phones.

Battery and Charging

The 5600mAh triple-cell battery supports wired fast charging up to 45W, wireless charging at 15W, and reverse wireless charging for accessories. Samsung's intelligent power management ensures efficient battery usage across the large foldable screen and high-performance chipset, delivering all-day usability under typical workloads.

Software and Features

Running Android 16 with One UI 8, the Tri-Fold integrates Samsung DeX, advanced multitasking with multiple apps on the large internal display, and gesture-based controls. Enhanced security and on-device AI features, including smart photo and video editing, real-time translation, and optimized app workflows, make this device a versatile productivity powerhouse.

Pros & Cons

    Pros:

    Immersive large display, high-end performance, premium build, advanced multitasking.

    Cons:

    Heavier than standard phones, high price (~$2500–$3000), glare in sunlight, limited fold positions.

Verdict

The Samsung Galaxy Z Tri-Fold is a true flagship foldable device, ideal for tech enthusiasts, creative professionals, and users seeking a tablet-like experience in a smartphone form factor. Its performance, display, and multitasking capabilities justify the premium price, though it may not suit users who prioritize portability or cost. For those looking for the most advanced foldable experience today, the Tri-Fold is a standout choice.
